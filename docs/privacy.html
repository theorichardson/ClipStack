ClipStack

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 24, 2026

ClipStack is a clipboard history manager for macOS developed by Theodore Richardson. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is stored, and what is shared.

What ClipStack accesses

ClipStack monitors your Mac's system pasteboard to record items you copy. This includes:

  • Plain text and rich text
  • URLs and web addresses
  • Images
  • File references
  • Items synced from your iPhone or iPad via Universal Clipboard

How data is stored

Your clipboard history is saved locally on your Mac in the app's sandboxed container at ~/Library/Containers/com.theorichardson.ClipStack/. Up to 500 clips are kept at a time. Images are stored as PNG files in a subfolder of that container. Older clips are automatically removed when the limit is reached.

ClipStack does not create a user account, does not sync your clipboard to any server, and does not use iCloud or any third-party cloud storage.

What is shared

Nothing. ClipStack does not transmit your clipboard contents or any other personal data to any server, third party, or external service. All processing happens on your device.

Analytics and tracking

ClipStack does not include any analytics, advertising SDKs, or tracking libraries. No usage data is collected.

Permissions

ClipStack runs in a macOS sandbox and does not request access to your contacts, location, camera, microphone, or any other system resource beyond the pasteboard.

Children's privacy

ClipStack does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy is updated, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated date. Continued use of ClipStack after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
